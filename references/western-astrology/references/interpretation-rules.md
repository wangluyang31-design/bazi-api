# Interpretation Rules

This file governs the **heuristic** layer only.

## 1. Reading order

Use this priority order unless the user asks for something else:

1. Sun
2. Moon
3. Ascendant
4. Mercury / Venus / Mars
5. Midheaven / House 10
6. Saturn / Jupiter
7. major aspects
8. outer planets as modifiers

## 2. Personality rules

Start from:

- Sun sign + house
- Moon sign + house
- Ascendant sign
- Mercury sign / house
- Mars sign / house

Then adjust with major aspects.

### Examples

- **Sun in Fire signs**  
  stronger visible will, self-expression, direct identity needs

- **Sun in Earth signs**  
  grounded identity, practicality, need for tangible results

- **Moon in Water signs**  
  greater emotional permeability, stronger instinctive sensitivity

- **Moon in Air signs**  
  emotions processed through reflection, naming, conversation

- **Strong House 1 emphasis**  
  self-definition and personal impact become central themes

## 3. Career rules

Start from:

- Midheaven sign
- House 10 sign / ruler logic if helpful
- planets in House 10
- House 6 for work style
- House 2 for value and earning style
- Saturn / Jupiter / Mars condition

### Examples

- **Midheaven in Earth signs**  
  prefers visible competence, structure, durable achievement

- **Jupiter tied to House 10 or Midheaven**  
  broader opportunity, growth through public role, teaching or expansion

- **Saturn strongly tied to Sun / Midheaven**  
  slower but more durable achievements; pressure, duty, standards

- **Mars in House 10**  
  competitive drive, visible initiative, stronger ambition or conflict around status

## 4. Relationship rules

Start from:

- Venus sign + house
- Moon sign + house
- Mars sign + house
- House 5 / 7 / 8 emphasis

### Examples

- **Venus in House 7**  
  partnership matters strongly; harmony and reciprocity are central

- **Moon in House 8**  
  emotional depth, intensity, stronger need for trust and transparency

- **Mars hard aspect Venus**  
  chemistry, friction, attraction through tension or different pacing

- **Venus hard aspect Saturn**  
  caution, reserve, seriousness, fear of rejection, loyalty themes

- **Venus hard aspect Neptune**  
  idealization, porous boundaries, projection risk, romance/imagination blend

## 5. Aspect rules

Treat aspect type as interaction quality.

- **Conjunction**: fusion, emphasis, concentration
- **Sextile**: cooperative opportunity, easier use with intention
- **Square**: friction, development through effort
- **Trine**: fluency, ease, natural talent
- **Opposition**: polarity, projection, balancing act

### High-value aspect patterns

- **Sun square/opposition Saturn**  
  self-pressure, duty, inhibition, high standards, earned authority

- **Moon square/opposition Saturn**  
  emotional restraint, self-protection, seriousness, delayed ease

- **Moon conjunction/square Pluto**  
  emotional depth, control issues, psychological intensity

- **Sun-Moon hard aspect**  
  internal tension between conscious goals and emotional needs

- **Venus-Mars soft aspect**  
  smoother desire / attraction integration

- **Venus-Mars hard aspect**  
  stronger charge, attraction + friction, pacing mismatch

## 6. Recommendation rules

Recommendations must connect to chart evidence.

Good format:

- name the tension
- name the chart evidence
- convert it into a behavioral suggestion

Example:

- "Because Sun square Saturn can increase self-criticism, build progress milestones and objective feedback loops instead of evaluating yourself only by final outcomes."

## 7. Safety / boundary rules

- Do not present astrology as certainty.
- Do not predict unavoidable disasters.
- Avoid medical, legal, or financial certainty claims.
- Avoid deterministic statements about relationships, pregnancy, death, illness, or fate.
- Frame outputs as tendencies, styles, patterns, and areas for reflection.

## 8. Output discipline

For each section, include:

- configuration summary
- strengths
- tensions
- recommendations
- evidence lines citing placements or aspects
